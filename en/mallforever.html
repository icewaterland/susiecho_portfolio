Overview

Brief

Heykorean provides a portal site for Korean living abroad, and it has offered a shopping experience intermittently within the portal site. After confirming the business feasibility through that experience, we decide to expand our commerce business by launching the e-commerce service MallForever. Design a commerce site that provides a satisfactory shopping experience by resolving user frustrations from the existing shopping experience and identifying users' needs.

This project was pending before starting the development due to changes in the business schedule and financial issues.

The Task

Offer a new product that integrates and improves the existing shopping experience for our members who use our portal site in the Northeastern US and Vietnam. Through this, increase the key indicators such as the trustworthiness of the portal site, loyalty, and member conversion rate, and expand the company's business field and profit model.

Problem

The shopping experience that was provided previously has arisen from intermittent promotions that sell products to users in the form of hot deals through free bulletin boards and event web pages on portal sites. By solving the problems that arise from this experience, provides an optimal shopping experience for users.

  • Alternative forms of shopping, such as bulletin boards and event web pages, provide a low-quality user experience.

  • Alternative types of shopping experience lower the credibility of portal sites.

  • The amount of communication that occurs during the purchase process and the frequency of VOC occurrence are high. It turns creates a high workload for internal employees.

  • The purchase conversion rate is low compared to the sales page visitation rate.

  • Existing shopping services do not give affects the effective indicators of our portal site, such as conversion rate and retention on portal sites.

Goal

  • Offer a new e-commerce site for users by integrating and improving the shopping experience within the portal site.

  • Expand business scalability by designing open market e-commerce.

  • Increase the efficiency of managers' work by reducing the incidence of shopping-related communication and CS.

  • Through the new e-commerce, increase the portal site registration rate and increase the inflow rate to other services of our company.

  • Activate the entire service by allowing spending the accumulated benefits from the portal service on the e-commerce site.

Proposal

Launch a new e-commerce site, MallForever, by integrating and improving all shopping experiences within the portal service. Through this, expanding the scope of activities and benefits of existing users. It does not only improve the shopping experience but also increases the satisfaction of the overall service experience. In addition, through MallForever, generate a new integrated user's subscription and connect to the company's various services. Moreover, expand the business area and develops a revenue model.

Empathise

Background

Our portal sites have provided shopping experiences in two ways. It is a form of offering a simple shopping website that can take advantage of membership benefits aperiodically, or a user or manager operating a group purchase through a bulletin board that can access at any time.

Hypothesis

After grasping the outline of the project, I found some points that seemed to be a problem then established the initial hypotheses for verifying them.

Research

Investigate user complaints that occur in the existing shopping service and define problems to be improved. In addition, I researched the market and potential customers to refer to the design of e-commerce products.

Market Research

1. Market Background

As of 2017, about 420,000 Koreans living in New York and Boston in the northeastern United States, and about 180,000 Koreans in Vietnam. I collected data on the method and scale of using e-commerce services that exist in the market. Moreover, gathered data on the use of the services we provide.

2. Competitor Analysis

Collected data that will help in the direction of the new product by conducting a competitor investigation focusing on the following items.

  • Research and analysis of the competitive service

  • Usability analysis such as structure and process of the competitive service

  • Analysis of the competitor service user reviews

  • Community-based e-commerce sites for Koreans do not exist in the northeastern United States and Vietnam, where are our target regions.

  • In the case of the Northeastern United States and Vietnam, where we will launch, there are no competitors that can purchase products using the membership benefits of the portal service.

  • Some Korean communities in the western US are operating portal site-based shopping malls. On a small scale case, sell products by themselves. On a large scale case, sell products in an open market format. The latter is similar to our ultimate goal.

User Research

By collecting data obtained from the existing shopping methods, identifies consumer needs and discovers insights on the direction of improvement.

The data and methods used in this research are as follows:

A) Sales through free bulletin boards: Analyzing data collected from previous methods

B) Sales through event page: Analyze data collected in the previous method + data operated once by the existing method during the project period

1. GA indicator analysis of event shopping website

  • The funnels were in the order of e-mail, the inflow rate of in-house advertisements through the main page of the portal site, Facebook, and external links.

  • The purchase conversion rate compared to the page inflow rate is in the 2.2% range.

  • The age groups of the buyers confirmed based on the membership registration information are in the order of 40s, 30s, 50s, and 20s.

  • Consist of 57% of women and 43% of men, shows a similar gender ratio.

  • The purchase rate of existing members is higher than that of new users of it.

2. VOC analysis

Grouped items by subject and the entered metadata were used to check the membership status, age, and gender of members.

  • Inquiries on how to order 38.2%

  • product inquiries 16.3%

  • Inquiries about returns 9.2%

  • Shipping Inquiries 32.6%

  • The others 3.7%

  • Trust in alternative methods such as bulletin boards is low, and there is confusion about the shopping process.

  • The ratio of VOC inquiries by age group was in the order of 40s, 30s, 50s, and 20s.

  • The age range of inquiry is wide, and the age ratio gap is not large.

3. A survey using bulletin boards within the portal site

It was conducted for portal site users for two weeks from the second week to the fourth week of October 2017, and 82 people participated. Some key items and their answers are given below.

  • What was the biggest factor in your purchase decision?

    • ‚Üí Friend recommendation, membership discount, low price, brand credibility of a portal site

  • If you gave up your purchase, for what reason did you give up?

  • What was the reason for the purchase after taking the inconvenience caused during the purchase(multiple responses possible)?

4. Empathise map

I tried to understand users more clearly by creating an empathy map for portal site users who conventionally purchased products.

5. User Interview

Gathered five users included in the below items, and interviewed them focusing on their shopping experience and general commerce purchase behavior. Interviews were conducted face-to-face, by phone, or in writing.

  • Users who has purchasing experience in our service

  • Users who said they would repurchase even through alternative channels

  • Users who have abandoned a purchase

  • Users who newly registered for purchasing on event shopping websites

Insights from this process include:

  • One of the main criteria for choosing an e-commerce site is reliability.

  • Existing users have brand awareness and credibility on the portal site, so it is relatively easy to connect to purchases.

  • For new entrants, the existing form of shopping is a big barrier that cannot be trusted.

  • Because it is overseas shipping, the shipping period is relatively long. Thus, users check the delivery status frequently.

  • Even young users, who are relatively accustomed to using e-commerce, feel uncomfortable and insecure about the our shopping service.

Define

Validated Hypothesis

Through above research, confirmed the hypothesis to be valid. And collected various complaints and wants from consumers in the process.

Derived the core needs of the users based on the difficulties experienced by the users and their wants.

The difficulties faced by users

  • Due to satisfied the event shopping, want to purchase again. But couldn't find it.

  • Purchasing on the bulletin board is inconvenient and difficult to believe, no matter how good the price.

  • Anxious because cannot check the contents such as the order history confirmation page or the payment history page.

  • Want to use the accumulated points for shopping, but might miss the benefits because don't know when the site open.

User Needs

  • Want to purchase reliable products at low prices whenever I need them.

  • Want to purchase goods through a reliable e-commerce site.

  • Would like it to be convenient to check completed purchase information such as order history confirmation, payment history confirmation, cancellation, and shipping status.

  • I want to get continuous benefits by using the membership and points obtained through activities.

Varified Problems

The problems identified through the research are as follows.

  • Alternative forms of shopping, such as bulletin boards and event web pages, provide a low-quality user experience.

  • Alternative types of shopping experience lower the credibility of portal sites.

  • The amount of communication that occurs during the purchase process and the frequency of VOC occurrence are high. It turns creates a high workload for internal employees.

  • The purchase conversion rate is low compared to the sales page visitation rate.

  • Existing shopping services do not give affects the effective indicators of our portal site, such as conversion rate and retention on portal sites.

Mission

To solve the verified problems, established some specific missions to be solved in the design process.

Target

Our target is overseas Koreans living in the United States and Vietnam, men, and women aged 20 to 55 years old. They differ in their proficiency in IT platforms according to their age groups. In this group, the primary target is the existing users of the portal site, and the secondary target is the new users.

The Platform

First, develop a web version that maintains consistency with the existing portal service and allows for relatively quick launch and release compared to native apps. Further, we will discuss the appropriate timing of the development of responsive web or native apps later.
